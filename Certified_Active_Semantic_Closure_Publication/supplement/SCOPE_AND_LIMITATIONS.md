# Scope and limitations

## Formal scope

The publication proves a generic impossibility theorem for visible continuation
aliasing and a generic closure-to-local-sufficiency theorem for the active
closure interface. It also proves, for the declared finite deterministic public
environment interface:

- an adaptive no-go over the full admitted class of public repair trees;
- an operational equivalence between certified repairability and a global
  action-resolving public tree;
- constructive synthesis from invariant-preserving composable pair separators;
- exact leaf posteriors for trees generated by a canonical exact compiler; and
- non-vacuity through one exact two-world instance.

It proves the complete positive repair chain for:

- one exact three-index finite realization; and
- one exact cumulative Boolean-stream realization at every natural stage.

The final aggregate also includes constructive foundational conservativity,
typed interventions, passive and visible-factored no-go results, and exact
quantized execution over a finite catalogue.

## Deliberate non-claims

The artifact does not claim:

- that every POMDP detector is complete;
- that arbitrary latent vector spaces automatically instantiate the interface;
- that the finite three-index theorem generalizes statistically;
- that the 697 examples cover continuous neighborhoods or unseen inputs;
- robustness to noisy, adversarial, delayed, or contradictory responses;
- optimal or minimal query count;
- minimal candidate patches for an arbitrary repair language;
- composition of simultaneous repairs or rollback;
- global closure of the open orbit at a finite time;
- superiority over external baselines or a completed benchmark campaign;
- historical priority for all individual concepts.
- that initial-state pair separability alone suffices without sequential
  composability;
- that posterior inclusion alone implies frame preservation;
- that candidate expressivity follows automatically from information closure;

## Why the theorem is still a solution

The target problem is constructive: characterize and exhibit systems in which aliasing is
identified, missing information is acquired, the representation is repaired
with provenance, old closures survive, local sufficiency is restored, and the
dynamics either terminates or continues with certified progress. The aggregate
theorem inhabits exactly that specification in closed and open settings.

Universality would require assumptions on observations, response reliability,
and the expressive power of a detector. Rather than hiding those assumptions,
the adaptive theorem makes separator composability, invariant preservation,
and candidate realization visible assumptions. Implementation-specific
obligations are discharged in the exact positive instance and in the two
complete finite/open trajectories.

## Empirical boundary

The quantized result is stronger than a floating-point test on its catalogue:
all relevant integer arithmetic and decision logic are recomputed by Lean. It
is weaker than a generalization theorem. The included scientific protocol is a
future falsifiable evaluation plan, not evidence that its hypotheses have
already succeeded.

## Publication language

Safe verbs are “formalize,” “prove,” “construct,” and “certify,” followed by the
scope shown in the claim–evidence matrix. Phrases such as “solves perceptual
aliasing in general,” “first ever,” “guarantees learned robustness,” or
“state-of-the-art” are unsupported by this release.
